My small village

kolazhy village


Kolazhy is a village and Gram Panchayat in the Thrissur district of Kerala. It lies about 7 km north of Thrissur city, making it a peaceful suburban area with easy access to the city. �

👥 Population

Population (2011 Census): 11,540 Literacy rate: 96.55%, which is higher than the Kerala average. The village has around 2,950 households. (Census 2011)

🌾 Geography

Total area: about 560 hectares. Nearly half of the land consists of wetlands and paddy fields. Nearby areas include Killannur, Pottore, and Viyyur.

🌴 Economy

Traditionally, the people of Kolazhy have depended on: Agriculture Daily wage labour Small businesses and jobs in nearby Thrissur city Today, many residents also work in education, healthcare, IT, and government sectors because of its proximity to Thrissur.

🛕 Culture

Kolazhy reflects the typical culture of central Kerala, with: Temples, churches, and mosques coexisting peacefully. Festivals such as the annual Pooram at the Thiroor Vadakkurumba Temple, which attracts many devotees.

🏫 Infrastructure

Kolazhy has: Schools and healthcare facilities. Good road connectivity to Thrissur. Strong digital governance—according to the Kerala Revenue Department, around 90% of village records and services have been digitized.

Why people like living in Kolazhy

Close to Thrissur city but quieter. Green surroundings with paddy fields and coconut groves. High literacy and good civic amenities. A blend of traditional village life and modern development.
